# What Is Thakali Dal Bhat? A Beginner's Guide

If momo is the dish people try first, Thakali dal bhat is the one that makes them
regulars. It is Nepal's comfort food, a complete meal on a single plate, and once
you understand it you will order it every time.

## What is dal bhat?

Dal bhat means lentils and rice, the everyday meal eaten across Nepal, often
twice a day. Dal is a spiced lentil soup. Bhat is steamed rice. On its own it is
simple. The magic is in what comes with it.

## What makes it Thakali?

The Thakali people come from the Thak Khola valley along the old salt-trading route
to Tibet, and they turned dal bhat into something special. A Thakali set arrives as
a spread: a darker, richer black dal, rice, a meat or veg curry, gundruk (fermented
greens), greens, and a row of pickles and chutneys.

It is balanced by design. Something sour, something spicy, something fresh,
something rich, all in one sitting.

## The best part: the refills

A proper Thakali set is bottomless. Servers come back around with more rice, more
dal and more sides until you tell them to stop. It is generous food, meant to
leave you full and happy, and it is one of the best value meals you can order.

## How to eat it

Mix a little dal into your rice, add a pinch of pickle, and work your way around
the plate so every bite is a little different. Use your right hand if you want the
traditional way, or a fork and spoon. Pace yourself. The refills are coming.

## FAQ

**Is Thakali dal bhat vegetarian?**
It can be. Most sets come with a veg or meat option, and the dal, rice, gundruk and
pickles are vegetarian by default.

**How spicy is it?**
Mild to medium on the plate, with the pickles bringing the heat. You control how
much you add.

**Why do they keep refilling my plate?**
That is the tradition. A Thakali set is all you can eat by design, so accept the
refills until you are full.
